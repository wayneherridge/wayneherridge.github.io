Provo Park Ward: Chapel

This chapel is probably one of my favorites. I loved the stained glass at the front, the impressive organ, and the chandeliers.

One article I found speaks of ward members decorating the chapel in greenery for Easter, Christmas, and sometimes in between. One member said, "It was like living in an Episcopalian church in northern England."

In case it's not obvious from the pictures, the pulpit is off-center--not by a lot; maybe 6 inches to a foot. I'm assuming it was intentional, but it was a little strange.

Historic LDS Architecture

Welcome to the Historic LDS Architecture blog! This blog studies significant architecture in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (also known as the Church of Jesus Christ, LDS, or Mormon Church). This includes temples, tabernacles, meetinghouses, and other buildings. Buildings that have been documented are listed below. There are several types of posts featured on this blog:

- Historic LDS architecture (see list below)
- Modern LDS architecture
- Destroyed LDS architecture
- Regular updates on LDS preservation projects and predictions on future ones
- Research on the history of LDS Tabernacles (for a Google Map of LDS Tabernacles, click here)
-Research on the history of LDS Stained Glass

Please ask for permission before using any photos. Feel free to contact me (see form below) with any questions, suggestions, or submissions related to Mormon Architecture. I'd love to hear from you!

This blog is updated every Sunday.
